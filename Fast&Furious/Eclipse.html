The Legend

This wasn't just a car; it was a statement. In The Fast and the Furious (2001), Brian O'Conner used this vehicle to infiltrate the street racing scene of Los Angeles.

The "9 Second Car" moniker became synonymous with the 4G63 engine's legendary tuning potential. With a shot of NOS, this car could hit 1320 feet in under 9 seconds, beating a Supra on the strip.

History & Legacy

From the Streets to the Screen

The Movie Car

The 1995 Mitsubishi Eclipse GSX became an instant icon after its appearance in The Fast and the Furious (2001). Driven by Brian O'Conner (Paul Walker), this green Eclipse with underglow neon lights became synonymous with the underground street racing culture of early 2000s Los Angeles.

The car featured extensive modifications including a turbocharged 2.0L 4G63 engine, all-wheel drive system, and a distinctive green paint job with neon underglow lighting that became a signature look for the franchise.

Real World Impact

Following the movie's release, Eclipse sales skyrocketed. The car became one of the most popular platforms for modification and tuning. The 4G63 engine's legendary status was cemented, and the Eclipse GSX became a highly sought-after vehicle in the tuner community.

Did You Know?

Multiple Eclipse cars were used during filming. The famous "9 second car" scene required precise timing and multiple takes. The neon underglow system used in the movie actually became a real aftermarket trend, though it was later regulated in many areas.
